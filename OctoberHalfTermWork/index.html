Gamers by Platform

The amount of gamers varies massively by platform - Mainly due to cost of the different platforms.

PC Gaming

  • ✅ Multi-use (except gaming)
  • ✅ Lasts longer than consoles
  • ✅(Usually) free Online Play
  • ✅ Usually more competitive
  • ❌ Large Upfront Investment
  • ❌ Requires good hardware

Consoles

  • ✅ Cheaper than PCs
  • ✅ Uses controllers natively
  • ✅ Keyboard/Mouse Support
  • ✅ Somewhat transportable
  • ❌ Paid Online Play
  • ❌ Not as flexible as PCs

Mobile

  • ✅ On-The-Go
  • ✅ Portable
  • ✅ Cheap (On Phone)
  • ✅ Games usually free
  • ❌ Full of advertisements
  • ❌ mainly basic / Idle Games

Other (Streaming)

  • ✅ No download
  • ❌Monthly Subscription
  • ❌High Bandwidth usage
  • ❌ Games aren't permanent
  • ❌ No offline play
  • ❌ Controller only

Failed Gaming Technologies

Sega Dreamcast

The Sega Dreamcast was released in 1999 and was the first internet-connected Console - Something which was way ahead of its time compared to other consoles on the market. Unfortunately, it's failures began with its predecessor when less units were sold.

The Dreamcast was eventually discontinued in the early 2000s due to the competition with the Playstation 2. Due to an economic recession in Japan, Sega was forced to cut the Dreamcast to survive.

Nintendo: Wii U

The Nintendo Wii U was released in 2012 as the successor to the Wii Gaming Console released in the mid 2000s. It is considered a failure due to the lack of support for Triple-A Games compared to PS4 or XBOX and it's lack of console sales.

The Wii U was succeeded by the Nintendo Switch in 2017 and has been a success worldwide.

Nintendo Powerglove

The Nintendo Powerglove was released in 1989 as a controller accessory for the NES (Nintendo Entertainment System). The Powerglove gained attention as it had virtual reality mechanics.

The Powerglove was a failure due to lack of games, those which did exists did not sell well.

The largest defining factor for the failure of the powerglove was its imprecise and difficult to use controls.

Gaming Habits

Teenagers/Young People (9-17)

Male Young People

Male Teenagers mostly play Action, shooters and sports games - These include Grand Theft Auto, Call Of Duty, FIFA and many others.

Female Young People

Female Teenagers play Mobile Games. This includes games such as Candy Crush, Idle Games and other mobile-based games.

Millenials (18-34)

Male Millenials

Male Millenials mostly play Action, shooters and sports games - Similarly to GenX. Their favourite games include God Of War, PUBG, Forza, FarCry and many others.

Female Millenials

Female Millenials play Casual and Action Games.. They enjoy games such as Candy Crush, but also games such as Tomb Raider or Assasins Creed.

Generation X (35-54)

Male Gen X's

Male GenX's mostly play Sports, Racing and Shooter games. Their favourite games include Forza, NBA, Call Of Duty and other shooter-based games like FarCry.

Female GenX's

Female GenX's similarly mostly play Casual Games (including Puzzle and Classic Arcades). Games Female GenX's play include Tetris, Pac-Man, Pokemon and other casual games.

Baby Boomers (55-64)

Male Baby Boomers

Male Baby Boomers mostly play Card, Puzzle and virtual board games. Their favourite games are Solitaire and Scrabble.

Female Baby Boomers

Female Baby Boomers similarly mostly play Card, Puzzle and virtual board games.. Their favourite games are Mahjong and Monopoly.
